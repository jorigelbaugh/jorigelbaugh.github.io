Who, Me?

Hey there! I'm Jori, a full stack developer who is passionate about connecting with people and using technology to improve how people interact with the world. I've worked on a variety of projects as a full stack mobile and web developer. I have experience with both front-end and back-end languages and frameworks, as well as querying languages, databases, and AWS tools. As a consultant, I've had the opportunity to learn and advise on development and testing best practices, lead workshops, gather requirements, and communicate technical concepts with end-users and stakeholders (a.k.a. translating developer jargon into normal human speak).

Prior to working at Atomic Object, I graduated in the spring of 2020 from Hope College with Bachelor's Degrees in Computer Science and International Studies. The combination of the two disciplines have helped me as a developer and consultant to navigate unfamiliar territory and team cultures, quickly learn technical languages, and engage differences with empathy.

When I'm not debugging code, I enjoy hiking, reading, writing, traveling, public speaking, and drinking absurd amounts of coffee. If you're interested in getting in touch, please reach out directly by e-mailing me at jori.gelbaugh@gmail.com, or connect with me on LinkedIn!

Readability Google Chrome Extension Developer

I worked with another developer to design and implement an award-winning software solution to help elementary and middle school teachers/para-pros assist students with learning and reading disabilities. The students that our clients worked with often experienced difficulty reading online articles, and our Google Chrome extension, Articulus, was designed to allow them to read more effectively and easily.

My partner and I researched readability metrics to develop and test an algorithm, the Carnahan-Gelbaugh metric, to compute the complexity of words and aid in finding appropriate synonyms to replace them. We communicated frequently with teachers and para-pros from Black River schools to ensure that our solution would be easy to use and access for our end-users.

Tech Stack & Tools:
